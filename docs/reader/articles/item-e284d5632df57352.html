不知情且不確定：LF Research 2025 開源 CRA 準備度現實

LF Research 的 2025 年報告聚焦開源社群對歐盟 Cyber Resilience Act 的認知與準備度。摘要指出，多數受訪者不熟悉 CRA，也不清楚合規期限與未合規罰則；報告建議製造商應在資安上扮演更主動角色，並增加對開源專案的資金與法律支援，以避免法規造成開源開發的負面影響。此資料較適合作為 CRA 議題的背景基準，而不是單獨成為最新主文。

Unaware and Uncertain: The Stark Realities of Cyber Resilience Act Readiness in Open Source

LINUX FOUNDATION RESEARCH

We’re investigating the impact of open source collaboration to solve the world's most pressing challenges.

About LF Research

ROI for Open Source Software Contribution

What is the benefit of contributing to open source? In this study, LF Research has tackled this question through the analysis of results from a survey and quantitative model on contribution ROI.

Participate in Our Research

Join the LF Research Forum and participate in our surveys and interviews to give back to your community and earn events & training discounts!

Interested in conducting research?

Review our prospectus for more information, and email us at research@linuxfoundation.org

This survey-based report investigates the awareness and readiness of the open source community to comply with the EU's Cyber Resilience Act (CRA). The report reveals significant knowledge gaps in the open source ecosystem that must be addressed to adequately comply with the new regulation while maintaining sustainable open source development.

The survey data shows that most respondents are unfamiliar with the CRA, are uncertain about compliance deadlines, and are unaware of non-compliance penalties. The recommendations developed from these findings include establishing a more active role for manufacturers in cybersecurity, increasing funding and legal support to open source projects, and developing guidance and best practices to prevent unintended negative impacts on development.

In response to this critical lack of awareness and understanding of the CRA, the Open Source Security Foundation (OpenSSF) developed the free Express Learning course Understanding the EU Cyber Resilience Act (CRA) (LFEL1001). We encourage anyone who develops software, or who manages development teams, to take a course such as this one to better understand the CRA.
